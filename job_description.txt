Google Data Scientist Job Description

Position: Data Scientist
Location: Mountain View, CA (or remote, depending on the team)

About the Role:
As a Data Scientist at Google, you will harness the power of data to solve complex challenges, drive innovative solutions, and influence business decisions at scale. Collaborating with cross-functional teams, you will design and implement models, experiments, and analyses that enhance Google’s products and services while delivering impactful insights to our global audience.

Key Responsibilities:
	•	Analyze and interpret large, complex datasets to identify trends, patterns, and opportunities that inform product and business strategies.
	•	Develop predictive models, machine learning algorithms, and statistical experiments to address high-impact business questions.
	•	Collaborate with engineers, analysts, and product managers to optimize workflows, improve data pipelines, and implement scalable solutions.
	•	Communicate findings and recommendations to stakeholders, translating complex data analyses into actionable insights.
	•	Design and execute A/B testing frameworks to assess the performance and impact of product features and initiatives.
	•	Contribute to building data-driven decision-making processes across Google by developing tools, dashboards, and automated reporting systems.

Qualifications:

Minimum:
	•	Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, Statistics, or a related quantitative field.
	•	Proficiency in Python or R and experience with SQL for data querying and manipulation.
	•	Strong understanding of machine learning techniques, statistical modeling, and experimental design.
	•	Proven ability to analyze large datasets and provide actionable insights.

Preferred:
	•	Master’s or Ph.D. in a quantitative discipline.
	•	Experience with big data technologies (e.g., Hadoop, Spark, BigQuery).
	•	Familiarity with cloud platforms such as Google Cloud Platform (GCP) or AWS.
	•	Strong communication skills with the ability to present technical concepts to non-technical audiences.
